Coastal communities in Georgia face greatest wildfire risk

St. Catherine's Island wildfire brings increased scrutiny on wildfire hazard potential

By Jessie Blaeser

June 27, 2022


Out of all natural disasters common in Georgia, wildfires do not typically top the list of concerns. But as a wildfire rages across St. Catherine’s Island along the state’s coast, concern for the preservation of some of Georgia’s most ecologically-diverse land grows.

Georgia’s coastal communities are at a greater risk than the rest of the state when it comes to wildfire hazard potential, according to data from the U.S. Department of Agriculture and U.S. Forestry Service.
